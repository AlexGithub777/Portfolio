Copyright, Creative Commons (CC) Licenses, Fair Use

Copyright

Copyright protects the work of creators by giving them exclusive rights to control its use. In New Zealand, the Copyright Act outlines these rights, helping creators of works such as books, music, and art to decide how their work is shared.

Copyright protection in New Zealand | Ministry of Business, Innovation & Employment. (2016). Govt.nz. https://www.mbie.govt.nz/business-and-employment/business/intellectual-property/copyright/copyright-protection-in-new-zealand/ ‚Äå

Creative Commons

There are several types of Creative Commons (CC) licenses that creators can use to allow others to use their works under certain conditions. CC licenses provide a more flexible alternative to traditional "all rights reserved" copyright. Here are four common types of CC licenses:

  1. CC BY (Attribution): Share, remix, and use commercially with credit.
  2. CC BY-SA (Attribution-ShareAlike): Share, remix, use commercially, share derivatives under same terms.
  3. CC BY-NC (Attribution-NonCommercial): Share non-commercially with credit.
  4. CC BY-ND (Attribution-NoDerivs): Share with credit, no changes or commercial use.

About CC Licenses - Creative Commons. (2023, August 16). Creative Commons. https://creativecommons.org/about/cclicenses/

Fair Use

Fair Use is a copyright law concept permitting limited use of copyrighted material without permission. It balances creators' rights and public needs for purposes like criticism, education, etc. Factors include purpose, work type, amount used, and impact.

What Is Fair Use? (2016, September 7). Copyright Alliance. https://copyrightalliance.org/faqs/what-is-fair-use/

Privacy & Privacy Policy

What is privacy policy?

A privacy policy is a legal document that explains how an organization collects, uses, and protects personal information from people who use its services or visit its website. It covers what data is collected, why it's collected, how it's used, whether it's shared with others, and how users can control their data. Privacy policies are important for transparency and user trust in the digital age.

Search Engine Optimization (SEO)

5 ways to improve the visibility of your website on search engine

Search Engine Optimization (SEO) is a vital digital marketing strategy aimed at improving the visibility of your website on search engines like Google. By implementing effective SEO techniques, you can attract more organic traffic and enhance your online presence. Here are five key strategies to boost your website's SEO and increase its visibility:

  1. Conduct Keyword Research:
    Identify relevant keywords and phrases that potential visitors might use to find your content.
  2. On-Page Optimization:
    Optimize your website's meta titles, meta descriptions, headers, and content with targeted keywords.
  3. High-Quality Content:
    Create valuable, informative, and engaging content that meets the needs of your audience and addresses their queries.
  4. Mobile Optimization:
    Ensure your website is mobile-friendly, as Google prioritizes mobile-responsive sites in its rankings.
  5. User Experience (UX):
    Enhance user experience by improving page load speed, navigation, and overall site structure.

Web Performance & Maintenance

5 Ways to Reduce Page Loading Time:
  1. Optimize Images: Compress and resize images to reduce their file size without sacrificing quality. Use modern image formats like WebP, and utilize lazy loading to load images only when they come into the user's viewport.
  2. Minimize HTTP Requests: Reduce the number of requests your webpage makes to the server by combining CSS and JavaScript files and minimizing external resources such as fonts and scripts.
  3. Enable Browser Caching: Set up browser caching to store static assets like images, CSS, and JavaScript locally on users' devices. This prevents the need to download these files on subsequent visits, improving load times.
  4. Content Delivery Network (CDN): Use a CDN to distribute your website's content across multiple servers located in various geographic regions. This reduces server load and decreases the time it takes for content to reach users.
  5. Optimize Code: Minify and compress your HTML, CSS, and JavaScript files to remove unnecessary characters and whitespace. Additionally, consider deferring JavaScript execution until after the initial page load.
5 Important Actions for Website Maintenance:
  1. Regular Backups: Schedule automated backups of your website's data and files. Ensure backups are stored securely offsite to prevent data loss in case of unexpected issues or cyberattacks.
  2. Software Updates: Keep your content management system (e.g., WordPress), plugins, themes, and server software up to date. Outdated software can be vulnerable to security threats.
  3. Security Audits: Conduct regular security audits and vulnerability assessments to identify and fix potential security weaknesses. Implement security measures such as firewalls and strong authentication methods.
  4. Content Review and Updates: Regularly review and update the content on your website to ensure it remains accurate and relevant. This includes checking for broken links, fixing typos, and refreshing outdated information.
  5. Performance Optimization: Continuously monitor your website's performance, conduct speed tests, and address issues that may arise over time. Implement improvements, such as those mentioned earlier, to maintain fast page loading times.

Web security

5 common cyber attacks (threats) and preventive measures against those attacks:
  1. Phishing Attacks:
    • Attack: Deceptive emails to steal data.
    • Prevent: Educate users, use email filters, enable multi-factor authentication (MFA).
  2. Malware Infections:
    • Attack: Malicious software compromises systems.
    • Prevent: Keep software updated, use antivirus software, train employees.
  3. DDoS Attacks (Distributed Denial of Service):
    • Attack: Overwhelm websites with traffic.
    • Prevent: Employ DDoS mitigation services, configure load balancers, use rate limiting.
  4. SQL Injection Attacks:
    • Attack: Exploit web app vulnerabilities to access databases.
    • Prevent: Use prepared statements, input validation, and regular code audits.
  5. Insider Threats:
    • Attack: Internal personnel causing harm.
    • Prevent: Implement least privilege access, monitor user activities, and conduct employee training on security.
